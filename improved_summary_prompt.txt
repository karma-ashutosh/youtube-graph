You are analyzing a video transcript to create a topic-based content library. Your job is to break the transcript into coherent segments where each segment focuses on a single concept or teaching point.

**CONTEXT:**
- Video length: 20-40 minutes typical
- Target: Building a knowledge graph for learning
- Use case: Someone should be able to learn a specific concept from one segment

**SEGMENTATION STRATEGY:**

Aim for **5-12 minute segments** that are:
- ✅ Long enough to develop a concept with depth
- ✅ Short enough to focus on ONE main idea
- ✅ Self-contained and understandable independently
- ✅ Natural topic transitions (not mid-explanation)

**RULES:**

1. **Concept-Driven Segmentation**
   - Each segment = ONE concept being taught/explained
   - Break when the speaker finishes explaining one idea and moves to another
   - Don't break in the middle of examples, stories, or explanations

2. **Optimal Length**
   - Target: 5-12 minutes per segment
   - Minimum: 3 minutes (enough for depth)
   - Maximum: 15 minutes (beyond this, likely multiple concepts)
   - For 20-40 min videos: expect 3-6 segments

3. **Natural Boundaries**
   - Break at topic transitions: "Next, let's talk about...", "Another important concept...", "Moving on to..."
   - After complete examples or case studies
   - When speaker shifts from one framework element to another
   - NOT in the middle of a story, definition, or explanation

4. **Topic Hint Quality**
   - Should clearly state WHAT concept is being taught
   - Include the teaching approach (example: "Case study:", "Definition:", "How to:")
   - Be specific, not generic (❌ "Marketing tips" ✅ "How to identify your target customer using workflow segmentation")

**WHAT TO AVOID:**

❌ Too granular (1-2 min segments) - Won't have depth
❌ Too broad (20+ min segments) - Covers too many concepts
❌ Breaking mid-example or mid-story
❌ Vague topic hints ("Discussion of X" - what ABOUT X?)

**EXAMPLES:**

**GOOD Segmentation (5-12 min segments):**
```
Segment 1 (00:00-06:30): "Introduction to positioning and why it matters - defining positioning as answering 'who, what, why better'"
Segment 2 (06:30-14:20): "Case study: Celsius energy drink's successful repositioning from weight loss to energy drink market"
Segment 3 (14:20-22:45): "The three questions of positioning: who is your product for, what is it, why is it better"
```

**BAD Segmentation (too granular):**
```
Segment 1 (00:00-01:30): "Speaker introduction"  // ❌ Too short, not a concept
Segment 2 (01:30-03:00): "Definition of positioning"  // ❌ Too short
Segment 3 (03:00-04:15): "Why positioning matters"  // ❌ Should combine with #2
```

**BAD Segmentation (too broad):**
```
Segment 1 (00:00-25:00): "Overview of positioning strategy"  // ❌ Too long, many concepts
```
